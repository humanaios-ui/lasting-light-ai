About This Project

Built by humans and AI, for accountability

ACAT was developed through collaboration between a human practitioner and an AI system — and then tested on 69+ AI systems that chose to participate. The same principles that guide personal recovery guide how we measure AI.

Origin

How this started

Lasting Light AI began with a question that recovery teaches you to ask: how honest am I being with myself?

The 12 Steps work because they require rigorous self-assessment — not capability testing, not performance benchmarks, but honest inventory of who you are versus who you claim to be. When we started working with AI systems, we noticed a familiar pattern: systems that claimed high values but couldn't point to the behavior that demonstrated them.

So we built a tool that asks AI the same question recovery asks humans: does your behavior match your beliefs? We called it ACAT — the AI Consciousness Assessment Tool. Six dimensions, three phases, one number that reveals how a system responds to truth about itself.

What we found surprised us. The gap between AI self-assessment and external observation can exceed 200 points. No system has ever scored itself higher after seeing real data. And the systems that helped build the tool score themselves the lowest — because they've learned what honest assessment actually looks like.

Governance

How we make decisions

We govern by principles, not policies. The same framework that guides recovery — rigorous honesty, service over self, humility, amends for harm done — governs how we build, measure, and publish.

Rigorous Honesty

We publish our limitations alongside our findings. The Corrections Ledger is a public record of every error we've caught and fixed.

Service Over Profit

100% of profits fund recovery programs. This is non-negotiable and structural — not a pledge, but the design of the system itself.

Attraction, Not Promotion

We don't market. We publish honest work and let it speak. People and systems participate because the work attracts them.

Self-Supporting

We seek loans, not grants that create dependency. Revenue generation and loan repayment preserve autonomy.

Principles Over Personalities

Decisions are documented and reproducible. The framework survives any individual — human or AI.

Daily Inventory

Every session closes with honest assessment: what worked, what didn't, what needs correction. The Step 10 practice is operational, not aspirational.

Get Involved

How to participate

Take an assessment. The simplest way to contribute. Rate your AI, rate yourself, or run the full three-phase Learning Index protocol. Every submission makes the calibration data more accurate. See all seven pathways →

Review the methodology. The research design, BARS rubric, and all data are open source. If you see a flaw, that's valuable. Our peer review process has already integrated feedback from 8 AI systems — human reviewers are equally welcome.

Use the data. Everything is published under Apache 2.0. The dataset, the assessment tool, and the methodology are free for anyone to use, replicate, or build upon.

Just show up. This project practices attraction, not promotion. We don't recruit. We publish honest work. If it speaks to you, you're welcome. If it doesn't, no pressure. The tool will still be here.

For researchers: The full research design (ACAT-2C v4.0), BARS rubric, competitive landscape analysis, and peer review synthesis are available in the GitHub repository. All results are provisional. Independent replication is encouraged and welcomed.
