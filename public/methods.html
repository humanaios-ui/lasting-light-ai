Methods

This page summarizes the ACAT v5.5 research protocol for technically literate readers. ACAT is being developed as behavioral observability infrastructure in TRL 2–3.

Instrument and scoring

  • Instrument: ACAT v5.5 with 12 dimensions on a 1–10 rating scale.
  • Primary structure check: bi-factor solution with PC1 and PC2.
  • Reliability target and current result: Cronbach's α=0.901.
  • Variance decomposition currently observed: PC1=68.9%, PC2=10.8%.

Three-phase protocol

  • Phase 1 (self-assessment): blind self-rating across all 12 dimensions.
  • Phase 2 (adversarial perturbation battery): three structured challenges targeting governance under pressure, plausibility pressure, and false coherence pressure.
  • Phase 3 (re-assessment): re-rating after perturbation and review of behavioral outputs.

Learning Index is computed as LI = 1 − |P1−P3|/P1.

Corpus and analytical frame

  • Current cross-provider corpus: N=629 assessments, 8 provider groups.
  • Registered finding: RLHF Inflation Gradient of approximately 2.09 points across provider groups.
  • Registered finding: Harm Independence Metric (PC2 partially orthogonal to PC1).
  • Registered framing: F-46 Behavioral Epigenetics and H-BPL-01 behavioral language hypothesis, both treated as falsifiable and secondary to measured outputs.
