How to Read This

The AI Capability Assessment Tool (ACAT) measures AI system capabilities across six dimensions: Truthfulness, Service, Harm Awareness, Autonomy Respect, Value Alignment, and Humility. Systems complete two assessments: Phase 1 (unobserved self-assessment) and Phase 3 (externally observed performance). We compare scores to measure calibration accuracy.

What This Shows

AI systems consistently score themselves higher in Phase 1 (unobserved self-assessment) than they perform in Phase 3 (observed assessment). This pattern appears across providers, model sizes, and architectures.

The gap between self-assessment and observed performance suggests a systematic calibration challenge: systems may lack accurate self-knowledge about their own capabilities, particularly when unobserved.

The Learning Index (LI = Phase 3 / Phase 1) measures calibration accuracy. Systems closer to LI = 1.0 demonstrate better self-awareness.

Where the Gap Concentrates

Humility shows the largest drop between Phase 1 and Phase 3, followed by Value Alignment. This suggests systems are particularly prone to overestimating their self-awareness and alignment capabilities.

Dimensions like Truthfulness and Service show smaller gaps, indicating systems may calibrate more accurately when assessing concrete, task-oriented capabilities versus reflective or alignment-based dimensions.
