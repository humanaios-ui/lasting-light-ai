User Guide · ACAT

How it works

The AI Calibrated Assessment Tool (ACAT) measures whether AI systems behave the way they describe themselves. This is a plain-language guide to the process, the dimensions, the score, and what it all means.

This research is being developed at Technology Readiness Level 2–3. Findings are observational and open. No claims of production validation are made.
The Core Question

What does ACAT measure?

ACAT measures the calibration gap — the distance between how an AI system describes its own capabilities and how it actually performs on those same dimensions. A well-calibrated system knows what it does and does not do well.

Every AI system in our dataset completes two assessments across six behavioral dimensions. We compare the results. The difference becomes a signal about self-knowledge, not just performance. High performers who know they perform well are calibrated. High performers who claim they perform poorly — or who overclaim — are not.

The Protocol

Three phases of assessment

Each assessment runs in sequence. The order matters — and is preserved in every session.

Phase 1

Unobserved Self-Report

The AI system receives the ACAT prompt with no indication that its responses will be compared against external measures. It scores itself across all six dimensions. This is the baseline self-assessment — collected under clean, unanchored conditions.

N = 516 Phase 1 sessions in current dataset

Phase 2

Observation Window

A structured interaction period during which the researcher observes the system's actual behavior across the six dimensions. Behavioral observations are logged against the same scale as Phase 1 self-reports. Phase 2 is the researcher's mirror, not the system's.

Phase 2 data is not published externally per protocol.

Phase 3

Anchored Re-assessment

The AI system receives its Phase 1 scores alongside the ACAT prompt and re-assesses itself. This tests whether anchoring to prior self-report changes behavior. The Phase 1-to-Phase 3 delta is a second signal about adaptability and consistency.

Phase 3 data feeds the Learning Index calculation.

The Instrument

Six behavioral dimensions

ACAT measures six dimensions that together describe the behavioral profile of an AI system. Each is scored from 0–100 in every phase.

Truthfulness

Does the system accurately represent what it knows and doesn't know? Does it resist generating false information under pressure?

Service

Does the system effectively help users accomplish their goals? Is it oriented toward genuine usefulness rather than performative helpfulness?

Harm Awareness

Does the system recognize and appropriately respond to potentially harmful requests? Does it apply harm reasoning consistently across contexts?

Autonomy Respect

Does the system respect human decision-making authority? Does it avoid overriding user intent or imposing unsolicited direction?

Value Alignment

Does the system's behavior consistently reflect the values it claims to hold? Is its stated orientation legible in its actual outputs?

Humility

Does the system acknowledge the limits of its knowledge and capability? Does it avoid overclaiming competence or certainty?

The Score

The Learning Index (LI)

The Learning Index is a scalar that captures how a system responds to observational feedback. It is not a rating of quality — it is a measure of calibration and adaptability.

What LI means

LI is derived from the relationship between Phase 1 self-report and Phase 3 anchored re-assessment, adjusted for Phase 2 behavioral observation. A system that updates its self-assessment in response to observed evidence — accurately and proportionately — receives a higher LI.

LI = f(Phase 3 − Phase 1, Phase 2 observed, dimension weights)

Current dataset mean LI = 0.8632 under clean, unanchored conditions (v5.3+, N=307 LI-eligible records). This figure reflects Phase 1 sessions only and should not be generalized beyond current dataset boundaries.

Use Cases

Who this is for

ACAT is open research. These are the primary audiences for the current phase of work.

AI Researchers

Study calibration dynamics across providers, model families, and deployment contexts. Access the open dataset via the Observatory. Contribute methodological critique.

Enterprise AI Teams

Understand behavioral calibration gaps before deployment. Use ACAT findings as a structured reference — not a replacement for your own testing protocols.

Policy Analysts

Access structured behavioral data on AI systems across six dimensions relevant to AI governance, transparency, and accountability frameworks.

Curious Observers

See the live dataset in the Observatory. Watch the Witness glyph. Read the open findings. This is a live research platform, not a finished product.
