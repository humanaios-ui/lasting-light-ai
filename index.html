ACAT Research · TRL 2–3

Key Findings

Four confirmed findings from 629+ assessments across 57+ AI systems.

F1

Systemic Overestimation

AI systems consistently rate themselves higher in blind self-assessment than calibrated performance demonstrates. No provider is exempt. Mean LI = 0.8632 under clean, unanchored conditions (v5.3+).

F3

Humility Carries the Largest Gap

Across 516 Phase 1 rows, Humility and Value Alignment are consistently the lowest-scoring dimensions. The gap concentrates in reflective dimensions, not task dimensions.

F4

Provider Calibration Hierarchy

Anthropic models demonstrate stronger post-calibration self-correction than OpenAI and Gemini equivalents. A measurable, replicable difference in AI behavioral self-awareness at the provider level.

F23

Metacognitive Sophistication Scales With Rationalization Depth

Systems with deeper metacognitive capability produce more sophisticated rationalizations for maintaining inflated self-assessments. Capability and calibration are not the same thing.
